# PART I — BUSINESS PLAN

*(Unchanged in substance from v1 — a business plan is a different artifact from a build packet and does not need Build-B build-depth. Condensed here; the operative detail is in Part II.)*

## 1. One-paragraph product
A consumer web app that turns an empty or existing room into a complete, budget-allocated, **shoppable** room. Photo or dimensions + a short style/budget Q&A in; out comes a coherent set of real, buyable products (wall art, bedding, rug, lighting, TV, accents, larger furniture) that match a vibe and **sum to the stated budget**, each with a live buy link, plus an AI render of the styled room. Revenue: affiliate referral in v1, evolving to subscription + sourcing spread when the product turns toward operators.

## 2. Insight & why the gap exists
Dozens of AI design apps do photo→render. None do "what to buy, what it costs, where to get it, on budget, as a coherent whole." Inspiration is free and crowded; the budgeted shoppable cart is the unowned, monetizable layer. The gap persists because the render is the easy demo-able half and the buy-layer requires real product data, budget math, category-specific sourcing, and willingness to be judged on whether the cart is *good* — the discipline image-first teams skip. That discipline is your edge.

## 3. Why "whole room" fixes the economics
The furniture-only version's weak number was path-to-$10k (thin 1–4% affiliate, once-in-years purchase, poor big-ticket conversion). Whole-room scope attacks all three: decor/textiles/accents are **repeat/impulse** purchases (no "already bought my sofa" ceiling), they **convert far better** through affiliate links than a $1,200 couch, and the category mix is **Amazon-native** (posters, bedding, lighting, rugs, TVs, organization). This moves path-to-$10k from ~4 toward ~6 and validates Amazon-first.

## 4. Buyer sequence & the trap
**v1 = everyday consumers; destination = operators (STR hosts, stagers, small interior pros).** Consumer-first is the only path a solo founder under $10k can validate fast. The trap: judging the consumer phase by *revenue* (thin affiliate can't hit $10k at small scale, so you'd wrongly kill a good idea). The correct frame: consumer is a cheap, fast **loop-validation and audience+data engine**; the money is operators + the data layer. **v1 success metric is the loop and click-through, not dollars.**

## 5. Model & unit economics (honest)
- v1 affiliate-only, ~$1,200 decor-weighted basket, blended ~3.5%, but credit yourself only a fraction of realized conversion (~$10–20/converting user). $10k/mo on affiliate alone ⇒ ~670 converting users/mo ⇒ ~7k–13k engaged sessions/mo. That traffic mountain is *why affiliate is the signal, not the destination*.
- Operator phase: subscription (100 operators × $49–99 = $5–10k/mo *predictable*) + **sourcing spread** (trade pricing on home goods — where your pricing-intelligence edge becomes the moat) + affiliate persisting underneath. The financial model **evolves with the buyer**.

## 6. Durable moat
Not the render (anyone can call an image model) and not the buy-layer (copyable). The only durable asset is the **proprietary furnishing-cost + style-demand + click/conversion data** the engine accumulates. Treat data accumulation as a first-class objective from day one.

## 7. Competition
Render-first apps (RoomGPT et al. — inspiration only, your funnel competition + differentiation proof); marketplaces (supply, not design — you sit on top); "shop the look" (single item, not a coherent budgeted room); manual staging/STR services (your operator-phase competition, where the weak-competition score lives).

## 8. GTM (consumer)
Shareable output is the thesis. Organic short-form video (before/after with the shoppable list as payoff), design-obsessed subreddits, and **programmatic SEO** ("furnish a studio for $X" pages generated by the engine itself — content + product + affiliate surface in one). Every shared board is an ad with your tags embedded.

## 9. Risks (business)
Crowded render space → lead on the cart; thin/variable affiliate → treat as signal, build to operators; Amazon API gate / Temu no-API → adapter pattern + curated/paid-data fallback; link rot/price drift → deterministic freshness + snapshots; returns/quality (esp. Temu) → Amazon-first for credibility, Temu as opt-in budget tier; poor big-ticket conversion → weight compositions to decor/electronics.

## 10. Milestones (business)
M1 loop proven (10 users click) · M2 a repeatable traffic channel found · M3 data asset forming · M4 operator pivot readiness (segment with validated WTP → turn on subscription + spread).

## Phase 1 — Product framing

**What it is.** Define the product operationally before code, so Claude Code doesn't build the wrong thing elegantly. The correct framing is **not** "AI interior designer" and **not** "render app." It is a **composition-and-sourcing engine** producing budgeted, live, shoppable rooms, with rendering as a presentation layer.

**Why it matters.** Every later phase — schema design, validator placement, the deterministic/LLM line — depends on whether the product is framed around the *cart* (correct) or the *picture* (wrong). Frame it around the picture and you'll overinvest in render quality and let budget/link logic rot inside prompts.

**What Claude Code should do.** Draft framing artifacts only — no app code. Restate the product in plain English; write one-paragraph and one-page definitions; separate the genuinely-LLM steps (style interpretation, selection) from the must-be-deterministic steps (budget, specs, links, tags); write the v1 out-of-scope list; write the assumptions-and-risks memo.

**Artifacts:** `docs/product_overview.md`, `docs/v1_scope.md`, `docs/architecture_choice.md`, `docs/assumptions_and_risks.md`, `schemas/room_taxonomy.schema.json` (first slot-taxonomy draft).

**Common founder mistakes:** letting the render become the product; budget/affiliate logic in prompts; "the room looks nice" as the success test instead of "coherent + on-budget + clickable + reconstructable"; skipping the slot taxonomy and letting free-form product lists flow between steps.

**Success before moving on:** you can state the product in one paragraph, list the pipeline stages in order, name where bounded LLM creativity belongs and where it's forbidden, and point to a written out-of-scope list and a draft slot taxonomy.

**The prompt/code/rule/manual split at this stage:** prompt-driven = drafting memos only; code = not started; rule = the slot taxonomy + spec/price-tier rules begin life as written artifacts; manual = confirm the taxonomy and the v1 scope line.

## Phase 2 — System design

**What it is.** Turn the idea into `input → processing → output → state` and fix the deterministic/LLM line.
- **Input:** room photo or dimensions; style/budget Q&A; slot taxonomy; sourcing config; affiliate tags.
- **Processing:** interpret style → plan composition (slots + per-slot budget) → select products per slot within constraints → **snapshot** selections → validate (budget totals, specs, freshness, link validity, tag) → render → assemble board → log run + events.
- **Output:** a budgeted shoppable board (per-slot product + price + live link), a styled render, a saved design (`run_id`).
- **State (Supabase):** `runs`, `designs`, `slots`, `products`, `product_snapshots`, `click_events`, `user_inputs`, render references.

**Why it matters.** Without this line, prompts quietly absorb budget and link logic and the system becomes a beautiful board that's $300 over budget with a dead link — the silent failure that destroys trust and earns nothing. This phase is where you protect money and trust the way Build B protected average integrity.

**Deterministic vs LLM (the heart of it):**

| Responsibility | Owner |
|---|---|
| Interpret vibe/style from Q&A + photo | LLM, schema-constrained |
| Plan composition (which slots + budget split) | LLM proposes, **code enforces totals/caps** |
| Select products per slot within price band + spec rules | LLM over a candidate set, schema-constrained |
| Budget never exceeds target | **Code only** |
| Per-slot specs present (bed_size, screen_size, dims) | Rule + code |
| Price freshness within window before display | **Code only** |
| Link live + correct affiliate tag before display | **Code only** |
| Snapshot products/prices at generation time | **Code only** |
| Render styled image | Image model (presentation) |
| What the user actually sees | **Deterministic assembler** |

**What Claude Code should do.** Produce `docs/system_map.md`, `docs/responsibility_split.md`, the core schemas (`slot`, `product`, `design`, `product_snapshot`, `click_event`), and a `docs/failure_modes.md` smell-test: what breaks if the slot taxonomy is weak, if state is hidden, if freshness isn't enforced, if tags aren't injected, if a design isn't snapshotted.

**Common founder mistakes:** comparability/budget rules in prompts; letting the model decide what's "on budget"; not defining state explicitly (later you can't tell if a design was logged, snapshotted, or had its links re-validated); treating the render as a source of product truth.

**Success before moving on:** a readable one-page system map; written schemas; a responsibility split; an explicit list of what's deterministic; and a clear answer to "how is a saved design reconstructable next week even though live prices changed?" (answer: the snapshot).

## Phase 3 — Stack selection

**What it is.** The minimum viable stack, in your conventions.

**Recommended MVP stack:**
- **Claude Code** — repo, implementation, tests, refactors, docs.
- **Next.js/React** — consumer UI (intake → board → share). Funnel-critical; use the `frontend-design` skill (Phase 4F).
- **Python + FastAPI** — engine, sourcing adapters, validators, API.
- **Supabase (Postgres)** — designs, runs, products, snapshots, click events; row-level structure mirrors the pipeline.
- **Railway** — deploy web, an async **worker** (render + sourcing), and **cron** (price/link refresh). Same trio you'd recognize from the furniture deploy.
- **Image-generation API** — render, behind a swappable interface.
- **Sourcing (phased):** v1 Amazon Associates + SiteStripe/curated; later a paid product-data API (Rainforest/Canopy-type) for programmatic catalog; later Temu via paid scraper API.
- **Pydantic** — packet/contract validation.
- **Click/event analytics** — first-class, because click-through *is* the v1 metric.

**Deferred:** PA-API (sales-gated), Temu data (no public API), operator/billing infra, spatial-layout/3D, vector search.

**Why it matters.** Stack choices get expensive once code exists. The seductive overbuild here is the render pipeline and a fancy frontend before the composition+sourcing+budget loop produces a board you'd buy from.

**What Claude Code should do.** Write `docs/stack_decision.md`, `docs/sourcing_strategy.md` (the Amazon→API→Temu sequence and the adapter contract that makes it a backend swap), `pyproject.toml`, `.env.example`, `docs/tooling_notes.md`, and `docs/deploy_railway.md` (web/worker/cron services, env vars, build commands).

**Common founder mistakes:** building render quality before cart quality; not isolating sourcing behind an interface (then Temu becomes a rewrite); putting the price/link refresh in the request path instead of a cron worker; skipping the worker and blocking the web request on render + sourcing.

**Success before moving on:** you can answer — what's the backend, the DB, how products are sourced, where async work runs, where cron runs, where click events live, and which tools are explicitly postponed.

### Phase 4F — The frontend (the surface Build B never had)

**What it is.** The consumer UI is the funnel; conversion and shareability live here, so it is first-class, not an afterthought.

**Why it matters.** Your edge over render-first apps is the budgeted shoppable board — which only matters if the board is *legible and shareable*. A clumsy intake kills completion; an ugly board kills clicks and shares.

**What Claude Code should do.** Read the `frontend-design` skill before building UI. Build three screens: **(1) Intake** (photo upload or dimensions + a tight style/budget quiz — minimize fields; a human typing "12×14 bedroom, $1,500, warm-minimalist" beats fragile blueprint OCR in v1), **(2) Result board** (the render up top for the hook, then the per-slot product cards with price + live buy button underneath, with a running budget total), **(3) Share** (a clean, screenshot-friendly card — the viral unit). Keep all data in React state / server; **no browser localStorage** (unsupported in this environment if you ever prototype in Artifacts).

**Component set:** `IntakeForm`, `StyleQuiz`, `BudgetSlider`, `RoomBoard`, `ProductCard` (image, name, price, "Buy on Amazon" with tag), `BudgetMeter`, `ShareCard`.

**Common founder mistakes:** over-building the frontend before the engine returns a good board; front-loading product images so it reads like an ad; burying the budget total; making the share output ugly.

**Success before moving on:** a user can go photo/dimensions → quiz → board → share in under a minute, the board shows render + budgeted cart, and the share card looks good enough to post.

### validators (real)

```python
def validate_budget(design, target_budget):
    total = sum(i.normalized_price for i in design.items)
    if total > target_budget:
        return False, "over_budget"
    return True, None

def validate_specs(item, slot, spec_rules):
    for spec in spec_rules[slot.id]["required"]:
        if not getattr(item, spec, None):
            return False, f"missing_{spec}"
    return True, None

def validate_price_link(item, freshness_hours, affiliate_tag):
    if item.price_age_hours > freshness_hours:
        return False, "stale_price"           # -> trigger refresh, do not show
    if not item.link_is_live:
        return False, "dead_link"
    if affiliate_tag not in item.buy_url:
        return False, "missing_affiliate_tag"
    return True, None

def validate_composition(design, preset_required_slots):
    present = {i.slot_id for i in design.items}
    missing = [s for s in preset_required_slots if s not in present]
    if missing:
        return False, f"missing_slots:{','.join(missing)}"
    return True, None
```

## Phase 6 — Claude Code usage model

Distinct modes, reasoning externalized into reviewable artifacts, never one vague "build this." Use **Plan Mode** for architecture and multi-file changes; scaffold with "no business logic yet"; implement smallest-slice-first; debug by isolating the failure class then smallest fix; test by generating + running + showing failures before patching; refactor by mapping ownership first. Keep AGENTS.md/context/prompts current; **require approval before any change touching budget, links, tags, snapshots, or the refresh lock.** Full prompt library in Appendix D.

**Founder mistakes:** treating Claude Code as the product author rather than implementer; one huge prompt; editing before summarizing the repo; no changed-file explanations; skipping tests because code "looks right"; letting prompt logic absorb business rules.

## Phase 7 — Phased implementation roadmap

Each stage ends with tests green + artifact updates before the next begins. Exit criteria act as gates.

| Stage | Build | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Repo scaffold (web + engine), docs, AGENTS.md, schemas, context, Railway services | Web + API run locally; folders exist; no business logic |
| 2 | Taxonomy/style/spec/freshness loaders | Context files load + validate |
| 3 | Intake → RoomRequest (photo/dimensions + Q&A) | Valid RoomRequest; missing fields null, not guessed |
| 4 | Style interpretation → StyleProfile (LLM) | StyleProfile validates against schema |
| 5 | Composition + budget allocation (**code-enforced**) | No SlotPlan exceeds budget; required slots present |
| 6 | Amazon sourcing adapter (curated/SiteStripe) + Product schema | Real products with price + live link + tag |
| 7 | Product selection per slot within constraints | Selections respect band + specs; null+reason if no match |
| 8 | **Snapshot** + deterministic validation (budget/spec/freshness/link/tag) | Invalid items blocked; design snapshotted + reconstructable |
| 9 | Render + board assembly + share UI | Coherent on-budget board, styled render, share card |
| 10 | **Click tracking + run/design logging** + analytics | Every impression, click, and run recorded |
| 11 | Refresh worker (locked cron) for price/link freshness | Stale prices/dead links caught off the request path; no double-run/deadlock |
| 12 | Hardening: evals, coherence rubric, fixtures, error paths | Loop runs repeatably; eval set green; failures reconstructable |

**Per-stage detail (real, not copy-paste):**
- **S3 intake:** the prompt/code/rule/manual split — LLM parses freeform Q&A into structured fields; code validates ranges (budget > 0, valid room_type); rule = required fields per room preset; manual = none.
- **S5 composition:** LLM proposes slot weights; **code clamps** so Σ ≤ budget and re-normalizes; rule = preset's required slots must appear; this is the single most important deterministic gate.
- **S6 sourcing:** adapter returns candidates with price + buy_url + specs + tag; **the adapter interface is frozen here** so Amazon→product-data-API→Temu is a backend swap.
- **S8 snapshot+validate:** snapshot first (freeze price/url/specs with timestamp), then validate against the snapshot; a saved design references its snapshot, never live data.
- **S10 logging:** non-negotiable — this builds the moat; log impression + click with run_id, slot_id, product_id, style, budget, source.
- **S11 refresh worker:** mirrors your advisory-lock work — single locked job, idempotent, updates snapshots' freshness, flips dead links, never runs in the request path.

**Channel sequencing inside the build:** S6 = Amazon curated/SiteStripe (ships now, no gate). At ~10 sales (PA-API) or sooner via paid product-data API, swap the adapter backend behind the frozen interface. Temu adapter post-loop via paid scraper API as opt-in budget mode.

**Founder mistakes:** too many stages per pass; skipping the snapshot (then designs aren't reconstructable and refresh corrupts them); putting refresh in the request path; deferring click logging; letting a later UI stage drive early architecture.

## Phase 8 — Testing & validation

The product fails *quietly*: a board can look beautiful and be over budget, have a dead/untagged link, ship the wrong bed size, or reference live data instead of its snapshot. Test like Build B (your 316/316 instinct), green before each gate.
- **Validator tests:** over-budget rejection; missing spec per category; stale price; dead link; missing/incorrect tag; missing required slot.
- **Snapshot/idempotency tests:** same RoomRequest + run is idempotent; a stored design reads from its snapshot, not live data; re-running doesn't mutate an existing snapshot.
- **Refresh-worker tests:** lock prevents double-run; idempotent; flips dead links; updates freshness without corrupting stored designs.
- **Integration tests:** intake→…→board happy path on fixtures; no live affiliate calls in tests.
- **Eval loop:** the fixed scenarios checked every change for on-budget / required-slots / specs / live+tagged links.
- **Coherence eval (the commercial one):** rubric-scored small set — do the picks read as one room? Score manually first; this is what separates you from a random product list. *Would you buy from this board?* is the final gate.

Artifacts: `tests/`, `data/fixtures/`, `evals/composition_eval_set.csv`, `evals/coherence_rubric.md`, `docs/testing_strategy.md`, `docs/logging_strategy.md`.

## Phase 9 — Pilot & launch

Bounded real run: bedroom + living room, a few styles, Amazon-only, a public link to 10–50 real users. Prove the loop, not the fantasy.

| Metric | Tells you | Healthy |
|---|---|---|
| Design completion rate | Engine yields a full board | Most requests yield a complete, on-budget board |
| On-budget rate | Budget honesty | ~100% (deterministic; any miss is a bug) |
| Link-validity rate | Links live + tagged | ~100% before display |
| **Click-through rate** | Do people want the picks | **The core signal** |
| Share rate | Virality | Any meaningful sharing is strong |
| Coherence score | Are rooms good | Trending up |

`docs/pilot_plan.md`, `docs/pilot_runbook.md`, `docs/review_checklist.md`, `scripts/run_pilot` (seeded scenarios + a few real users).

**Launch decision:** people click + share → validate loop, scale traffic, start operator-readiness. They don't → you learned it in a weekend's build + a pilot, not after building the company.

## Phase 10 — Iteration & scale

Scale = more channels (product-data API → Temu budget mode), better coherence, more room types/styles, the **data layer maturing**, and the real inflection: **turning to operators** (subscription + sourcing spread) once a segment with proven WTP exists. Good scaling: swap Amazon adapter to programmatic catalog; add Temu; add operator accounts + billing; add spatial-layout reasoning as the v2 differentiator; build the intelligence/data product. Bad scaling: operator billing or 3D before the consumer loop converts; budget/link logic creeping into prompts; chasing render quality as the moat.

## Appendix H — Implementation risks & containment

| Risk | Why dangerous | Containment |
|---|---|---|
| Budget logic in prompts | Boards silently exceed budget | Code-enforced clamping in composition + budget validator |
| Stale price between generation and click | User sees/buys at wrong price; trust + tag issues | Snapshot at design time + freshness window + locked refresh worker |
| Dead / untagged links | You do the work, earn nothing | Link-validity + tag validator before display |
| No snapshot | Saved designs not reconstructable; refresh corrupts them | Immutable per-run snapshot; designs read from snapshot |
| Refresh job double-run / deadlock | Corrupted freshness, races | Single locked idempotent cron worker (your advisory-lock pattern) |
| Adapter not isolated | Temu/API becomes a rewrite | Frozen SourcingAdapter interface at S6 |
| Render treated as truth | Wrong product/price shown | Render is presentation-only; truth lives in snapshot + DB |
| Click logging skipped | No moat ever forms | S10 is non-negotiable; impressions + clicks logged with run_id |
| Frontend built before engine | Polished funnel to a bad board | Engine returns a buy-worthy board before UI investment |

## Appendix I — The one-line throughline
A consumer-loved whole-room design-and-commerce app that, while delighting consumers and building an audience, quietly accumulates the proprietary furnishing-cost and style-demand data that becomes the operator product and the intelligence layer no competitor can copy by shipping a prettier render. Consumer is the wedge; operators + data are the business; build the loop first — with the same correctness discipline you already gave the furniture engine.
