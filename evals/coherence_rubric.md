# evals/coherence_rubric.md
# Manual coherence rubric — scored before each eval loop gate.
# The commercial question: "Would you buy from this board?"

## How to score

Review the assembled board (product names, prices, style, room type) without
looking at the style profile used. Score each dimension 1–5.

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Style coherence (1–5)
Do the products feel like they belong in the same room?
- 5: Every item reinforces the same vibe; nothing clashes.
- 3: Mostly coherent with one or two off-note items.
- 1: Items feel randomly selected; no shared aesthetic.

### 2. Budget believability (1–5)
Does the overall price feel right for the stated budget?
- 5: Every item is appropriately priced for the budget tier; no wildly over- or under-priced outliers.
- 3: Mostly appropriate; one item feels out of place.
- 1: Prices are confusing or clearly wrong for the budget.

### 3. Spec accuracy (1–5)
Are the required specs correct for the room and user input?
- 5: Bed size, TV size, rug dimensions all match stated room inputs.
- 3: Most specs correct; one minor mismatch.
- 1: Specs are clearly wrong (e.g. twin bedding for a queen room).

### 4. Buy intent (1–5)
After seeing this board, would you click "Buy" on at least one item?
- 5: Yes, immediately.
- 3: Maybe, after checking alternatives.
- 1: No — picks are unappealing, too generic, or untrustworthy.

### 5. Completeness (1–5)
Does the room feel finished? Are there obvious missing pieces?
- 5: Room feels complete and liveable.
- 3: One or two things feel missing but the core is there.
- 1: Room feels bare or incoherent as a whole.

---

## Gate threshold

A board must average ≥ 3.5 across all five dimensions to pass the coherence gate.
Any board scoring < 3.0 on buy intent is flagged for prompt or sourcing review,
regardless of the overall average.
